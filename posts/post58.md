🏗️ Don't Just Learn Bioinformatics — Build Things While You Learn

The biggest mistake I see people make when learning bioinformatics: they study tools, read tutorials, and watch lectures — but never build anything of their own.

Learning without building is like studying swimming from a book. You need to get wet.

Here's what "building while learning" looks like in practice:

📊 Instead of just running the Seurat tutorial → analyze YOUR lab's data with it
🌐 Instead of just reading about R Markdown → write a real analysis report
🔧 Instead of just understanding pipeline concepts → actually build one, even if clunky
📝 Instead of just consuming content → share what you learn (LinkedIn posts, GitHub, lab presentations)

Every time you build something real, you discover questions you didn't know you had. Those questions drive deeper learning.

The side effect? You have a portfolio. When someone asks "what have you done with bioinformatics?" you can show them, not just describe it.
