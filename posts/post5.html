FindSubCluster: Targeted Subclustering in Seurat

Deep-dive into specific cell clusters with FindSubCluster() for granular cell-type resolution without global over-fragmentation.

The Challenge

While FindClusters() identifies broad cell populations, sometimes you need deeper analysis of specific clusters. But increasing global resolution can fragment unrelated populations.

The Solution: FindSubCluster()

FindSubCluster() applies subclustering only to clusters of interest (e.g., Cluster 0), creating meaningful subdivisions (0_0, 0_1, 0_2) while preserving other data.

Key Advantages

  • Granular analysis without over-fragmentation
  • Improved cell-type annotation for specific populations
  • Preserved cluster identity for unrelated populations

Usage

Applies subclustering only to clusters of interest, creating meaningful subdivisions while preserving the rest of your data—the perfect middle ground between global clustering and fine resolution.
