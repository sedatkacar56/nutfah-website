Open Current Directory in File Explorer with One Command

💻 A Simple Command That Opens windows in Windows: >start .

Sometimes, it's the small commands that make life easier. In Windows Command Prompt, typing:

start .

That is it!!! Easy Peasy!! Go ahead and try!!! Without trying, u cannot used to it!

instantly opens the current directory in File Explorer. No clicking, no searching — just a fast way to jump to the folder you're already in.

For those working on Linux or macOS, the equivalent is:

xdg-open .

or

open .

depending on the system.

These tiny shortcuts may not look flashy, but they save seconds every time you move between the terminal and your files and those seconds add up when you're working on complex data or coding projects. Whether it's navigating project folders for a Seurat single-cell analysis, reviewing scripts, or launching visualizations, a quick "start ." or "open ." keeps your workflow seamless.
