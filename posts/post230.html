ATS 2026 Observations — Splitting by Condition, Annotation Transparency, Integration Choices

🧬 Observations from single-cell research presentations at ATS 2026 in Orlando.

On visualization: splitting by condition is one of the most important visualization choices for revealing biological differences. Nonsplit UMAPs often obscure what the biology is actually showing. Researchers who split their data by condition immediately communicated more clearly.

On annotation transparency: researchers who displayed marker feature plots directly on their posters demonstrated robustness and clarity in cell identification. Showing your evidence builds trust in your annotations.

On data integration: merging or integrating datasets should be based on context. Presenting unintegrated samples without justification — without cross-sample comparison — raises questions that could be avoided with one additional step.

On difficult cell types: myofibroblasts remain particularly challenging to identify given marker overlap with smooth muscle and fibroblast populations. Many posters lacked detailed justification for myofibroblast calls.

On presenters: researcher expertise and confidence significantly influenced poster quality and discussion value. The person matters as much as the data.

🧠 In-person scientific exchange remains irreplaceable for these kinds of contextual lessons.
