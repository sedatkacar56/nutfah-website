🧬 Do you really know what separates ChIP-seq, ATAC-seq, and CUT&RUN? They all map genomic regions. They all tell you something about chromatin. But they see the genome differently.

🔹 ChIP-seq

ChIP-seq is like placing a GPS tracker on one specific protein and asking: "Where exactly are you standing on the genome?" It zooms in on one target at a time. Precise. Focused. Protein-specific. But the signal can be noisy — like looking through fog.

🔹 ATAC-seq

ATAC-seq doesn't care about who is standing there. It asks: "Which doors in the genome are open?" It gives you a global accessibility map — a city map of open streets — regardless of who is walking through them. Wide-angle view. Genome-wide openness.

🔹 CUT&RUN

CUT&RUN is like ChIP-seq — but with a sharper lens. Instead of breaking everything apart and sorting it out later, it cuts precisely where the target sits and releases only the relevant fragments. Cleaner signal. Lower background. Less starting material. More surgical.

🎯 The difference isn't about better or worse. It's about perspective.
ATAC-seq shows where chromatin is open.
ChIP-seq shows where a specific protein binds.
CUT&RUN shows the same — but with more precision and efficiency.

Last but not least

A simple analogy: imagine the genome as a city. ATAC-seq shows you which streets are open to traffic (accessible regions), while ChIP-seq shows you exactly where a specific person is standing on those streets (protein binding).

🧠 Same genome. Three ways of looking at it. Three levels of resolution. Understanding the method changes how you interpret the biology.
