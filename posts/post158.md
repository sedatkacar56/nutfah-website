🧠 Don't Just Learn Bioinformatics — Make It Count

A/ It's tempting to try learning everything in bioinformatics — every tool, every package, every paper. But without direction, you'll just burn out. Instead, start by asking questions:

❓"Why is gene X expressed in these cells — and what if it showed up elsewhere?"
❓"Does RNA expression really match protein levels?"
❓"Which isoform is dominant — and does it matter functionally?"

Let your curiosity drive your learning. Let questions shape your path.

B/ Learning without a goal is like reading about fishing without ever casting a line. Don't just absorb — build. While you're studying or even working (if your boss allows it), turn your insights into output:

🧰 A GitHub project
📝 A blog post
📄 Better yet, a review or article
💬 Or just helping someone else with their analysis

Make something tangible — so months later, you can look back and say, "That wasn't in vain."

C/ Producing solidifies what you learn. You start thinking deeper:

🔍 "What does this really mean?"
🔄 "How can I reuse or apply this in the future?"
🧩 "How does this piece fit into the broader puzzle?"

Without creation, knowledge becomes temporary — in one ear, out the other.

D/ Long story short:

❌ Don't just learn randomly.
✅ Learn purposefully. Create intentionally. Make your learning permanent — not passive.

And if you keep catching yourself using that fishing analogy… hey, maybe you're onto something. 🎣
