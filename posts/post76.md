I dived into it with basic question/answer style: 🔷 What is kinit? kinit is the Kerberos "login" command: it asks for your password (or uses a keytab) to fetch and cache a Ticket-Granting Ticket (TGT) for your principal. Once you've run kinit username@REALM, you can access Kerberized services (HDFS, secure NFS, etc.) without retyping your password. Check your active ticket with klist. 🔶 What is Kerberos? Kerberos is a network authentication protocol designed to let two parties (like you and a service) prove their identities to each other securely over an insecure network. 🔷 What is the origin of the word "Kerberos"? The name "Kerberos" comes straight from Greek mythology. In ancient Greek, κέρβερος (Kerberos) was the three-headed dog who guarded the entrance to Hades, preventing the dead from leaving and the living from entering. When the MIT developers designed the protocol in the 1980s, they chose "Kerberos" as a playful nod to its role as a "guard" for network services. 🔶 What does it mean to "cache a Ticket-Granting Ticket (TGT) for your principal"? What is a Kerberos principal? A "principal" is the identity you're authenticating as - typically your username plus a realm, e.g. alice@EXAMPLE.COM. It's the account name that gets verified by the Kerberos Key Distribution Center (KDC). What is a TGT? Once you prove your identity (by entering your password or using a keytab), the KDC issues you a TGT. This is a time-limited, cryptographically-signed ticket that says "yes, this principal really is who they claim to be." What does "caching" that TGT involve, and why is it useful? After you get the TGT, kinit stores it locally (by default in a file like /tmp/krb5cc_). This cache lets you re-use your TGT to request service tickets for other Kerberized resources (HDFS, NFS, etc.) without re-entering your password - until that TGT expires. 🔷 What is REALM? In Kerberos, a realm is the administrative domain over which a single KDC has authority. It's typically uppercase (e.g. EXAMPLE.COM) and often matches your DNS domain, but it doesn't have to. All principals (users and services) within that realm share the same KDC and trust chain. 🔶 What is klist? klist is the Kerberos command that displays the tickets you've obtained and cached (your TGT and any service tickets). It shows the principal names, ticket start & expiry times, and the location of your credential cache: bash $ klist For keytabs, use klist -k to list their contents. #singlecellRNAseq #singlecelldatastorage #kerberos #securelogin #linuxshell
