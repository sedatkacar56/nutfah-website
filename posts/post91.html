Understanding PCA Eigenvalues in Single-Cell RNA-seq — Q&A

🚀 Understanding PCA means understanding variance — and that means understanding eigenvalues in Single-Cell RNA-seq! Q&A Session Hey single cellers:) Let's break down together key concepts that often confuse in simple way:

1️⃣ What are eigenvalues in single-cell RNA-seq analysis?
🔹 Eigenvalues represent how much variation each principal component (PC) captures from the high-dimensional gene expression data.
✔️ A higher eigenvalue = a PC capturing more important biological differences (e.g., cell types, states).

2️⃣ Is an Elbow Plot the same as a Scree Plot?
🔹 Yes! Both plot eigenvalues (or related metrics) against PCs to find the "elbow point" where variance explained levels off.
✔️ In Seurat (R package), ElbowPlot() creates this visualization.

3️⃣ In an Elbow Plot, does the Y-axis (standard deviation) represent eigenvalues?
🔹 Not directly — but they are closely related! Standard deviation is the square root of eigenvalue.
✔️ Higher standard deviation → higher eigenvalue → more variance explained.

4️⃣ Is DimHeatmap in Seurat a heatmap of eigenvalues? What about bar plots?
🔹 DimHeatmap shows genes × PCs — not eigenvalues.
✔️ X-axis: Cells (ordered by PC score) | Y-axis: Genes (loading heavily on the PC).
🔹 Bar plots are great for variance explained:
✔️ X-axis: Principal Components | Y-axis: Variance Explained (%).
🔹 Cumulative Variance Plot:
✔️ X-axis: PCs | Y-axis: Cumulative Variance Explained (%).
🔹 Elbow/Scree Plot (Seurat):
✔️ X-axis: PCs | Y-axis: Standard Deviation (sqrt of eigenvalues).

5️⃣ Is Variance Explained (%) the same as Eigenvalues?
🔹 Almost — they are connected:
✔️ Eigenvalue = raw variance captured by a PC.
✔️ Variance Explained (%) = (Eigenvalue ÷ Sum of all Eigenvalues) × 100.

🔍 In short: Eigenvalues show raw variance. Variance Explained (%) shows normalized variance. Elbow and Scree plots help decide how many PCs to use.
