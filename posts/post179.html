You Found the Clusters — Now Where Are They? Spatial Transcriptomics Answers That

🧬 You found the clusters in your single-cell RNA-seq data. Nice. But now what? You labeled: • Endothelial • Fibroblast • Immune • Smooth muscle • Maybe even a proliferative subcluster

Great. But here's the real question: 👉 Where are they?

Because cells don't live in a vacuum. They live in tissue. They interact. They organize. They form structure.

Clustering tells you who they are. Spatial transcriptomics tells you where they live. And that changes everything.

• Is your proliferative EC cluster lining remodeled vessels? • Are fibroblasts enriched in fibrotic niches? • Are immune cells forming spatial gradients? • Are cell–cell interactions location-dependent?

This is where spatial becomes a playground. Not just expression matrices. Not just UMAP islands. But tissue architecture. Neighborhood structure. Spatial autocorrelation. Microenvironment logic.

Single-cell answers: "Who am I?" Spatial answers: "Where am I — and who is around me?"

And biology lives in that second question. 🚀

The future isn't single-cell or spatial. It's integration.
