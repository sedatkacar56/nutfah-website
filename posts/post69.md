Sometimes, motivation matters more than experience.

A young researcher once tried to switch fields. He reached out to a lab that worked in his dream area. The lab head asked a few questions — he answered honestly, but his knowledge was limited. Still, his motivation was sky-high.

He didn't get the position. Worse, he never received a reply to his follow-up message. That silence left a deeper mark than rejection itself.

He prayed with a broken heart, still hoping to grow.

Later, he started a job he wasn't excited about — but it turned out to be exactly what he needed. It taught him the very skills he had hoped to learn.

And then something remarkable happened: He ended up analyzing data for the same lab that once didn't take him in — through their clients.

Not every closed door is a loss. Some rejections are redirections in disguise.
