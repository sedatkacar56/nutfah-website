Wet Lab Origin Single-Cell Analyst — Experience Builds Confidence

A/ I'm a wet lab-origin single-cell RNA-seq guy. Most of my time goes into analyzing unique single-cell datasets — not public databases, but data straight from live experiments, often untouched before.

B/ I repeat similar steps, but each time becomes faster and more efficient — always tweaking, improving, and elevating. Sometimes limited learning is enough; practice often teaches more than studying everything.

C/ When a weird error shows up, there's no panic — you've seen it before, handled it before. No need to search forums or ask AI. That's where confidence builds.

D/ When someone asks for an analysis, it doesn't feel overwhelming — because it's familiar. When things feel unclear or too difficult, the mind hesitates, overthinks, and procrastinates. But when there's experience, you just get to work without wasting mental energy.

E/ You often deliver more than expected — sometimes three insights when only one was requested. You start asking questions beyond the standard, finding deeper biological meaning. You master the process — and you trust your own functions.

F/ Instead of relying on outsourcing, you build your own efficient way of working. Yes, you still use tools when needed, but only when they truly add value.

G/ To me, experience means:
• a stash of working, reusable code
• understanding the flow — not just writing blindly
• enjoying every part of the process, even debugging
• and always linking results to meaningful biology

H/ With AI and endless tools, it's impossible to learn everything — but it's fully possible to master what you've learned and make it a continuous, enjoyable process.

I/ That's what leads to discovery. New algorithms. Smarter workflows. Thinking beyond the box — by mastering every part within it.
