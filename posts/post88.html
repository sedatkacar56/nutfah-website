scDblFinder() Parameters for Better Doublet Detection

Three key scDblFinder() parameters to improve doublet-calling accuracy:

clusters — Provides cell-type labels so artificial doublets form between distinct biological groups rather than randomly, making heterotypic doublets easier to identify.

clustCor — Adds correlation features to cluster profiles to detect "mixed identity" cells that correlate with multiple cell types simultaneously.

samples — Restricts detection within capture batches/wells rather than across them, as true doublets arise within single captures.

Combining all three gives cleaner calls and fewer false positives.

However, when testing scDblFinder with various parameter combinations (default, with samples, and with both samples and annotations), I couldn't see much robust separation between detected doublets and singlets in UMAP visualizations. The algorithm's efficiency in practice is something worth questioning and validating.
