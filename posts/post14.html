cat(), print(), and message() in R: Choose the Right Output Function

Three Output Functions Explained

cat() — Raw text output without formatting. Has no soul.

print() — Structured output with quotes and metadata. Understands objects.

message() — Quiet logging that can be suppressed with suppressMessages(). The quiet one.

Quick Comparison

FunctionBest UseSuppressible
cat()Status updatesNo
print()Values/objectsNo
message()Non-intrusive infoYes

💡 Ideal for long pipelines: Use message() for background logging that users can suppress if needed.
