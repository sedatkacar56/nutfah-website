Aspera Connect vs FileZilla — A Two-Day Upload Battle I Finally Won

🚀 Aspera Connect vs. "Filla Villa" — A Two-Day Upload Battle I Finally Won

Today I tried to upload a single spatial transcriptomics sample — something I've done many times with single-cell FASTQs without any issues. But this time… the system estimated 2 weeks to upload ~200 GB of data. Two weeks. For one sample.

I spent two days trying to understand what was going wrong. I checked my files, my connection speed, different transfer settings, even FileZilla limits — nothing worked.

Then I went back to the instructions from the data submission portal one more time…

And that's when I noticed something I had never used before: 👉 Aspera Connect

At first, I thought it was a paid service. The website mentions trial limits (2 weeks or 50 GB max), so I assumed I couldn't use it for scientific submissions. I almost gave up — until I realized that Aspera Connect is free when used for uploading to scientific databases after manuscript submission.

So I installed it, used the command-line uploader, and…

💥 200 GB uploaded within a couple of hours. Not days. Not weeks. HOURS.

Sometimes the solution is in the documentation the whole time — we just skip it because we're used to our old workflow. Elhamdulillah — lesson learned. Always reread the instructions. Always look for the alternative path.
