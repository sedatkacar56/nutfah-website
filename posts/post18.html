Combining Annotation Columns: Using coalesce()

#QUESTION: Single cell or R experts here please:) You merged or integrate at least pre-annotated 2 seurat objects (each has its own annotated column in metadata). However, those columns in metadata do not work anymore with different cell barcodes and samples added. You want one single annotated UMAP. An idea popped up and You thought "Why dont I merge or combine the premerged/preintegrated annotation columns per Seurat?". You know each can only annotate one single Seurats barcodes, all other cells are "NA". Here is what would be the function to use: will combine those 2 columns, will have no NA, it will not overlap each other and u will safely visualize your annotation with UMAP or TSNE. There can be other answers but I will give only one solid answer that worked for me. Do not forget, no second codes or secondary manipulation. Just with one function, keep combine those columns in one!!! I am waiting your answers. #SingleCellSeq #Seurat #metadata #annotation #UMAP #tsne #2Ddimentionalreduction #PCA #ElAlim

The answer to the question above:

The Answer: coalesce() from dplyr! 🎉 It takes two or more columns and returns the first non-NA value for each row. Perfect for combining pre-annotated columns after merging Seurat objects.

library(dplyr)

# After merging two pre-annotated Seurat objects:
# seurat_merged@meta.data$annotation_obj1 has NAs for cells from obj2
# seurat_merged@meta.data$annotation_obj2 has NAs for cells from obj1

seurat_merged@meta.data$combined_annotation <- coalesce(
  seurat_merged@meta.data$annotation_obj1,
  seurat_merged@meta.data$annotation_obj2
)

# Now visualize with the unified annotation
DimPlot(seurat_merged, group.by = "combined_annotation")

✅ No loops, no ifelse, no secondary manipulation. Just one function. coalesce() fills in NAs from the first column with values from the second — exactly what you need when each annotation only covers one object's barcodes.
