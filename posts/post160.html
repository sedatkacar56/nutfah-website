Don't Chase Every Bioinformatics Package — Stick with Seurat

A/ 🚨 You don't need to chase every new bioinformatics package or method to do great science. The shiny tools will always be there. Mastery is what moves you forward.

B/ If you already know a tool deeply — stick with it. I use Seurat instead of Scanpy for 90% of my single-cell work. Not because I'm behind, but because I know it like the back of my hand. That familiarity means I can debug, reshape, adapt — without hesitation. Jumping tool to tool just piles up confusion. You're not analyzing anymore — you're babysitting black boxes.

C/ Yes, keep learning. Yes, explore. But don't let FOMO hijack your pipeline. If something takes others 10 steps and takes you 3 — that's your edge. Sharpen it. Use it. Fast doesn't always look fast — but it feels that way when you're in command.
