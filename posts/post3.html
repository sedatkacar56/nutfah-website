Rat Lung Atlas: 140K Cells at Unprecedented Resolution

We are proud to present the first and most detailed and robust single-cell RNA atlas of the rat lung at an unprecedented resolution of ~140k cells. This dataset represents a major step forward in respiratory research, providing an invaluable reference for the rat lung, which has been largely absent in the literature.

What Makes This Atlas Unique?

  • ✅ Comprehensive mapping of five major cell groups: Epithelial cells, Endothelial cells, Mesenchymal cells & Immune Cells.
  • ✅ Detailed annotation of T cell subclusters, categorized by activation status and functional roles, offering a deeper look into adaptive immunity.
  • ✅ Identification of transitional cell states, shedding light on dynamic changes in lung biology.
  • ✅ Potential for cross-species annotation: This rat lung reference can also aid in annotating mouse and human single-cell datasets using orthologous genes via Ensembl.

Current Status

This dataset is actively being used in our ongoing research projects for cell annotation and is proving to be a powerful tool for studying lung biology.
