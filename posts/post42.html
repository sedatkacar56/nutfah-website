Single-Cell Lung Bias: Why Epithelial Cells Are Underrepresented

🫁 A Critical Bias in Single-Cell Lung Research: Epithelial Underrepresentation

Something I noticed at ATS 2025 that isn't talked about enough: most single-cell lung datasets severely underrepresent epithelial cells.

Why does this happen?

🔴 Technical reasons:
- Epithelial cells are large and fragile — they lyse during dissociation
- Standard tissue dissociation protocols favor immune and stromal cells
- Epithelial cells tend to aggregate, getting filtered out in QC

🔴 Biological consequences:
- We're missing the cells that actually line the airways
- Disease-relevant epithelial subtypes (AT1, AT2, club cells) are undersampled
- Conclusions about "lung biology" may actually reflect immune biology

✅ How to address this:
- Use epithelial-enrichment protocols before single-cell capture
- Try single-nucleus RNA-seq (snRNA-seq) — nuclei survive better
- Apply computational deconvolution to correct for bias
- Use FACS sorting to enrich for EpCAM+ cells before library prep

This is a fundamental limitation of current lung single-cell atlases. Knowing the bias helps us interpret data correctly.

Have you noticed this in your lung datasets? 👇
