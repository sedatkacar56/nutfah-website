Two Concerns About AI Agents — Repetition and Biology

There are two concerns that occupy my world these days. If you feel like listening, you're always welcome to lend an ear.

1️⃣REPETITION (Hands-on Experience)

AI is incredible. Tasks that once took days or hours now take minutes. AI thinks fast, assists well, and so far—everything looks great.

However, there's something deeper we shouldn't ignore.

If you jump into AI without getting your hands dirty, without blending into the real, messy experience, you may use it—but you won't truly digest it. And if you can't digest it, you can't fully comprehend it. And without comprehension, you can't create anything truly tangible to improve it.

Knowing the basics matters. Titles matter less. No matter who you are, mastery only comes from hands-on repetition—again and again. Repetition is where new ideas are born.

2️⃣ BIOLOGY: You can't know what isn't there

Think about Deep Blue and Garry Kasparov. Deep Blue couldn't consistently beat Kasparov in the past—why? Because there simply wasn't enough data to train on.

Fast forward to today. Modern chess engines give humans virtually no chance. Why? Because they've been trained on massive datasets and can evaluate an enormous number of variations.

Now apply this to biology.

In biology, what we haven't discovered is far greater than what we have. AI can only work with existing knowledge. That's why polishing our minds matters. Learning how to think better. Learning how to ask better questions—even "silly" ones.

There is an enormous amount of biological data out there. AI can help us explore it—but only if we truly understand what we're doing.

Use AI. But don't outsource your thinking.
