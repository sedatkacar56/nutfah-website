Marker Knowledge Separates a Coder from a Scientist

🧬 Biological marker knowledge is not optional for single-cell RNA sequencing analysis — it is essential. "Marker knowledge separates a coder from a scientist."

Understanding that mRNA levels don't always match protein expression is critical for proper cell annotation. Transcription and translation are distinct biological processes. Different sequencing platforms capture genes differently. Gene dropout and naming inconsistencies occur across species and technologies.

Blind reliance on automated tools without validation leads to misannotation.

What deep marker knowledge enables: • Spot incorrect annotations quickly • Detect rare cell populations • Recognize artifacts versus genuine biological signals • Provide stronger biological interpretation beyond clustering results

Running a bioinformatics pipeline is something anyone can do. Genuinely interpreting the data — understanding why a gene is expressed in a specific cell, recognizing when an annotation is biologically implausible — requires deep biological understanding.

That's the difference between pipeline operators and true analysts.
