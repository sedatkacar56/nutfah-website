💭 You Become What You Do

Academic life often feels like a paradox: the work is meaningful, but the path is uncertain. You pour years into research that may or may not pan out. You sacrifice comfort for curiosity.

But here's what I've learned: you become what you repeatedly do.

🔬 Every analysis you run builds your intuition
📖 Every paper you read shapes your perspective
💻 Every function you write strengthens your craft
🤝 Every collaboration expands your thinking

The PhD isn't just training for a job — it's training your mind to approach hard problems systematically. That skill transfers everywhere.

When I started learning bioinformatics, I failed constantly. Code didn't run. Results didn't make sense. Papers went over my head. But I kept going, one commit at a time.

Now those struggles are strengths.

If you're in the middle of a hard stretch in academia right now: you're not stuck. You're becoming.

What has academic life taught you that surprised you? 👇
