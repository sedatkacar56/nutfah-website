You cannot truly master many things at the same time.

People often claim they know something—but confidence alone doesn't equal mastery. In fact, when someone speaks with absolute certainty, it often means they've only scratched the surface.

The deeper you dive into a subject, the more you realize how much you don't know. New layers appear. New questions emerge. Endless ways to expand, refine, and improve become visible.

That's where real mastery begins.

True learning starts when:
You stop asking only basic questions
You start questioning the core principles
You ask questions few people think to ask
You repeat the process again and again

Discovery without understanding the underlying methods is difficult—sometimes possible, but rarely sustainable.

Mastery takes time. It takes consistency. It takes resisting boredom and continuing to engage your mind deeply.

Stay patient. Stay focused. Keep learning. TRUST IN GOD.

The future is yours.
