#RealPostdoc_Stories 1: 🧪 Back to the Darkroom — in 2023! 🧪 Yes, this is real film-based Western blot, and yes, it's from August 31, 2023. 📸⬇️ (Images from that day below) While deleting old folders from my OneDrive archive, I stumbled upon these scanned films — and suddenly, all the memories came rushing back. I couldn't delete them. Instead, I decided to post them here to make them permanent and remember those days. When I joined the lab, I expected digital imagers and automated exposure settings. But to my surprise, the PI preferred the classic method — film development, handwritten labels, timing exposures by hand, and of course, working in a darkroom. At first, I thought: "Is someone joking?" I couldn't even see properly in the dark! And asked my colleague, how are you able to see properly? But after doing frequently, my eyes adjusted — and so did I. 😅 Despite the old-school feel, I came to respect the method. We scanned the films to computer, used Ponceau staining to confirm equal loading, and didn't just normalize by β-actin unless the bands were clearly consistent. Different labs, different approaches — but the discipline and care remain the same. Has anyone else recently revisited older techniques like this?
