Are you surprised when the same file shows up bigger after copying on HPC? Like in my case: 'du -sh' said 26 GB vs 15 GB, which is almost twofold. - Hidden files, sparse formats, or temporary blocks can cause this jump. Actually, you heard me right. The size increased even though the content didn't. Don't worry: the data is intact, it's just the way scratch filesystems work. If you want the true logical size, use du -sh --apparent-size .
