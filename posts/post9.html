Understanding Algorithms: Go Beyond Just Using Tools

Hey! #WetLabScientists with #ComputationalBiology #skills!!! This is the way of #DISCOVERY!!!

-For those of us coming from a wet lab background, it's time to go beyond just using bioinformatics tools—we need to understand the algorithms behind them!

-Techniques like PCA (Principal Component Analysis), UMAP, t-SNE, K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN), clustering algorithms, and machine learning models are widely used in modern biology, from single-cell transcriptomics to structural biology. But blindly trusting these methods in every analysis without questioning their assumptions might limit our ability to uncover something truly novel. 🔬

Why does this matter? Understanding the mathematics and logic behind these methods allows us to:

✅ Identify biases in our data.
✅ Make informed choices when selecting algorithms.
✅ Avoid over-reliance on default parameters.
✅ Open doors to groundbreaking discoveries by thinking beyond pre-packaged solutions.

-I'm not saying this to those from computer science or mathematics—they already master these techniques. But for those of us focusing on biological insights, integrating computational thinking into our workflow is essential.

-This post is inspired by one of the best explanations of PCA I've come across. Let's keep learning and challenging the way we analyze data!

-Ever felt lost applying bioinformatics tools without understanding the math behind them? 🚀💡
