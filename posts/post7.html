How to Choose the Right Number of PCs in Seurat

PCA reduces high-dimensional transcriptomic data by capturing variation patterns across genes. Each PC represents an axis of variation.

The ElbowPlot Method

Use ElbowPlot visualization to select optimal PCs: X-axis = number of PCs; Y-axis = variance explained.

What Happens at Different PC Levels?

  • 2 PCs: Minimal structure, snake-like patterns
  • 5 PCs: More definition but unstable
  • 10 PCs: Captures major variance
  • 15 PCs: Refined clusters
  • 20+ PCs: Minimal changes indicating stability

The Golden Rule

Too few PCs → Lose biological signal
Too many PCs → Add noise without major improvements
Sweet spot: Where variance capture stabilizes
