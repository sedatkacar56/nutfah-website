Extending Kerberos Ticket Lifetime for Long Data Transfers

Every day brings new learning opportunities in HPC! 🎫💻 Lost so many hours to failed transfers due to expired Kerberos tickets - never knew I could extend them! Having constant issues with authentication timeouts killing my large file transfers mid-process. Always restarting genomics data transfers when tickets expired after just 1 hour. So frustrating! 😤

My breakthrough came with this simple command:

$kinit -l 24h -r 72h username

Data lifetimes can be set to 24 hours instead of that painful 1-hour default! Under this approach, tickets become renewable for up to 72 hours too. Looking back, if 24 hours still isn't enough, just run kinit -R to renew instantly!

Instead of assuming defaults were requirements, I should have checked what was actually allowed. Lesson learned: I wrongly assumed the 1-hour limit was an unchangeable institutional policy. Learning that my institution allows much longer ticket lifetimes - I just never asked! Actually discovered the solution was simpler than I thought!

Here are the key insights from this experience:
✅ Always check what your system actually allows vs. what it defaults to
✅ For large data transfers, request appropriate ticket lifetimes upfront
✅ Use klist to monitor your ticket status
✅ Don't assume defaults are requirements!

Now my genomics data transfers run smoothly without authentication timeouts. Do not forget check existed tickets or ticket history by $klist and convert your files tar.gz format not to get trouble later!!!
