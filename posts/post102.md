💡 Quick R Tip — Locate Your Installed Packages Easily! If you use R regularly, you've probably noticed that we usually have multiple library paths on our computers. And sometimes, it's easy to get confused about where exactly our packages are installed. Knowing this matters because: ✅ It helps you manage different R versions or shared library paths. ✅ You can troubleshoot missing or conflicting packages more easily. ✅ It prevents issues when switching between RStudio, HPC, or command-line R sessions. Before checking a specific package, start with this command: In R: .libPaths() 🧭 Example output: [1] "C:/Users/username/Documents/R_shared_library/library" [2] "C:/Users/username/AppData/Local/R/win-library/4.3" [3] "C:/Program Files/R/R-4.3.2/library" Then, to locate a specific package, run: In R: find.package("Seurat") 📍 Example output: [1] "C:/Users/username/AppData/Local/R/win-library/4.3/Seurat" Small details like this can make a big difference when debugging or managing multiple R setups! #DataScience #Informatics #HPC #SingleCellRNAseq #Analytics #RStats #RProgramming #Automation
