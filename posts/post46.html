From Wet Lab to Bioinformatics — My Transition Story

🔬➡️💻 From Wet Lab to Bioinformatics — My Transition Story

Three years ago, I was spending most of my days at the bench — flow cytometry, cell culture, Western blots. Now I spend my days writing R code, building analysis pipelines, and exploring single-cell data.

Here's what I wish someone had told me at the start:

✅ Your biology knowledge is a SUPERPOWER in bioinformatics
You understand what the data actually means. Computational biologists without wet lab experience often miss the biology — you won't.

✅ You don't need a CS degree
I learned R by doing. Start with analysis of your own data — the motivation keeps you going when the learning curve gets steep.

✅ The transition takes time — be patient
It took me 18 months before I felt fluent. Every expert was once a beginner asking "why won't this code run?"

✅ Find your community
LinkedIn, Bioconductor forums, Seurat GitHub issues, Twitter/X — the bioinformatics community is incredibly helpful.

✅ Build things, not just knowledge
The best way to learn is to analyze real data, solve real problems, and share what you find.

If you're in wet lab wondering about the transition: it's worth it. The skills are complementary, not competing.
