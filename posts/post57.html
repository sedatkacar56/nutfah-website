Your Biology Background Helps You Steer Bioinformatics Better

🧬 Your Biology Background Helps You STEER Bioinformatics Better

Something I notice in the field: the bioinformaticians who make the biggest impact usually have deep biology knowledge, not just coding skills.

Here's why biology knowledge is a multiplier in computational work:

🎯 You ask better questions
A pure programmer sees "this cluster has high expression of gene X." A biologist asks "does that mean these are dying cells or stressed cells? Does the marker make sense anatomically?"

🔍 You catch biological nonsense faster
When your DE analysis shows GAPDH as the top differentially expressed gene, you immediately know something is wrong with the normalization. A programmer might not.

🗺️ You navigate the literature better
You know what's already known, what's controversial, and what a novel finding actually means. This shapes your entire analytical approach.

🤝 You collaborate more effectively
When you can talk to clinicians and wet lab scientists in their language, you get better experimental designs and more impactful insights.

Code is learnable. Deep domain knowledge takes years to build. If you have it, it's your competitive advantage — use it.

The best bioinformaticians I know think like biologists who happen to code, not coders who happen to study biology.
