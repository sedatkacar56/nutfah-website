How to Read Chord Diagrams in Cell Communication Analysis — ATS 2026

🧬 At ATS 2026, I learned to interpret chord diagrams used in cell communication analysis — not from the keynote, but from a conversation in front of a poster. That's often where the real learning happens at conferences.

How to read a chord diagram: • Arc segments represent genes (ligands or receptors), colored by cell type • Ribbons show ligand-receptor interactions between cells • Diagram titles indicate whether a cell type is a sender or receiver • Gene names help identify whether a molecule is a ligand (sender side) or receptor (receiver side) • Ribbon thickness indicates interaction strength — thicker = stronger interaction

These visualizations synthesize complex biology from tools like CellChat, LIANA, and NicheNet. A single chord diagram can represent hundreds of ligand-receptor interactions across dozens of cell types — compressed into one interpretable figure.

🧠 The visualization is dense by design. Once you know what each element means, you can extract enormous biological meaning from a single image.
