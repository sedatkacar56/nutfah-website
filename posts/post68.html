Running Out of Storage? It Might Be FILES, Not SPACE

🚨 Running Out of Storage? Not So Fast... It Might Be FILES, Not SPACE! 🧠💾 This week I learned something that made me scratch my head — and then nod in appreciation of system admins. I checked my storage usage on our HPC cluster:

sda → 13% used (6.8T / 48.8T) ✅
sda files → 101% used (25,474 / 25,000) ❌

Wait... I have tons of space left — but Im locked out because I hit the file (inode) limit? 🤯

That led me down a rabbit hole of:
Why we have file count quotas (not just size)
Why sda has such a tiny limit compared to slate or scratch
And what sda even means (SCSI Disk A? Really?)

Turns out:
✔ File quotas protect system performance
✔ sda is optimized for large files, not millions of logs or scripts
✔ And yes — you can run out of files before running out of bytes!

So next time your cp fails mysteriously, check both disk space AND file count.

📦 Pro tip: Compress, batch, or shift to scratch if you are hitting limits.
