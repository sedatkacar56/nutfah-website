Endothelial Cells: More Than Just Hollow Tubes

"Endothelial cells? Come on... Just empty space with the same tiny lines? No difference!" — What I used to think!

How deep you go determines how much you discover. With today's tools, we can reach materials and insights faster than ever. But here's the thing: It's not about speed — it's about depth.

The Transformation

When I first joined my current department, my PI mentioned subsets of endothelial cells. I thought, "Subsets? Endothelial cells are just... endothelial cells. Hollow tubes, right?" Honestly, I didn't even want to focus on them. They felt boring.

But once I truly started working with them... 💥 It was like stepping into a whole new world. Endothelial cells are deeply involved in almost every disease. They're the connective bridges between all organs — critical, dynamic, and anything but simple.

The Lesson

"The feeling of knowing everything often comes from knowing very little." The more I learned, the more I realized how much I didn't know. And that's the beauty of science.
