FeaturePlot Color Scales: Interpreting Zero Expression

The Question

What's the difference between two FeaturePlots in terms of gene expression? At first glance, they look quite different — but here's the twist: there's almost no real difference in the data itself.

What's Happening?

Graph 1: Just one single cell expressing the gene. Only that cell appears purple, rest are gray. Perfectly intuitive.

Graph 2: NONE of the cells express the gene—all values are zero. But oddly, the plot still shows cells in purple.

Why?

FeaturePlot() applies a default color scale regardless of whether there's any true expression signal. While the "all-zero" case is more common, both can mislead interpretation.

⚠️ Key Takeaway

Always check the underlying values or adjust the color scale—especially when creating plots for collaborators or publications. This is especially tricky with split.by, where color scales may be hidden or inconsistent.
