🧬 Switching from Wet Lab to Bioinformatics? You're NOT Starting from Scratch. You're actually starting 1–0 ahead.

A/ If you're transitioning from a wet lab background into bioinformatics, don't think you're starting from zero. Not at all. Your biology experience brings depth—especially when it comes to interpreting complex results.

B/ As a bioinformatics scientist, I don't just run pipelines, process barcoded matrices, or generate UMAPs. That's just the technical layer. Of course, these steps can be challenging too—especially when the pipeline is new or you've never done that type of analysis before. But the real, ongoing challenge? Understanding what the data means—and deciding what to explore next.

C/ This is where my biology and histology background play a key role. My histology knowledge helps me sense where certain cells should be located, how they relate spatially, and whether the annotations "feel" right based on their known biology. Without that foundation, I'd have to ask others things like: "Is this meaningful?" "What should I focus on?" "Does this pattern make sense?" But now I can often answer those questions—or at least frame them much better.

D/ Yes, setting up workflows takes effort at first. You'll get stuck. You'll Google a lot. You'll rewrite code 10 times. But with tools like ChatGPT and other AI copilots, the entry barrier is lower than ever.

E/ Still, AI can't replace scientific reasoning—at least not yet. Why? Because many answers you're looking for don't exist in the literature. And even when they do, they're often buried in contradiction or uncertainty. That's where your brain steps in.

F/ AI isn't a chef handing you a ready-made dish. It's a sous-chef—helping you prep, clean, and streamline your work. One day it might become the chef. But we live in the present—and for now, real insight is still in your hands.

G/ So if you're shifting from wet lab to bioinformatics:
🔬 Don't downplay your past experience.
🧠 It's now part of your scientific toolkit.
🚀 Use it to ask better questions, uncover hidden patterns, and move with meaning.
