Basics matter.

There are many chess applications and bots out there now—some of them can easily beat humans. Since the Kasparov–Deep Blue match, it's clear that no human can truly challenge top chess engines anymore.

But would you play chess for a long time if you were only pretending to play—just copying the moves of super chess bots?

If you did, what would your motivation be?

Motivation is the driving force of learning, persistence, and improvement. If you don't understand what you're doing, it becomes much harder to stay motivated.

Just like in chess, vibe coding can be useful up to a point. But without understanding, it eventually leads to a dead end: no fuel, no fun, no clarity—just struggling with simple errors and endless copy-paste fixes.

Understanding what you're doing keeps you on track. It makes you more robust, more confident, and—most importantly—allows you to enjoy the process (at least sometimes).

So what do you want?

Do you want to sprint, run out of breath, and stop?

Or do you want to run a marathon—with enough air to keep going?

Value yourself. Use new technologies, but at the same time, know what you're doing. In the long run, that's what makes you the winner.
