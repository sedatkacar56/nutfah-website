"Coding Is Dead" — A Response to UW's CS Enrollment Drop

💻 "Coding Is Dead" — My Take on the UW CS Enrollment Drop in the AI Era

I've seen the headlines: University of Washington CS enrollment dropping, students avoiding coding because "AI will do it." Some people are saying coding is a dying skill.

I disagree. Here's my perspective as someone in bioinformatics:

🔑 Coding is not dying — generic coding is changing

What's changing:
- AI handles boilerplate code, syntax lookup, repetitive patterns
- Junior-level "write this CRUD app" coding is being automated
- The bar to produce basic code has never been lower

What's not changing:
- Understanding WHY code does what it does
- Debugging complex, domain-specific problems
- Designing algorithms for novel scientific questions
- Knowing when results are biologically wrong vs. computationally wrong

In bioinformatics specifically: the hardest problems aren't coding problems. They're biological interpretation problems that require code to answer.

A bioinformatician who understands single-cell biology + can use AI-assisted coding is MORE valuable now, not less. You move faster AND go deeper.

The UW enrollment drop might reflect something else: students see AI and panic, rather than adapting. The ones who adapt will build the field.

Is coding dead in biology? Absolutely not. It's more democratized and more powerful than ever.

What's your take? 👇
